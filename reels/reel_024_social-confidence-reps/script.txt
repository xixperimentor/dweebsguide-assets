Not too long ago, I was chatting with someone I really liked.

The conversation hit a rough patch, and instead of just rolling with it, I panicked and clammed up. I left feeling embarrassed and frustrated. Then, I realized something important during my next interaction. I paid closer attention to how quickly I bounced back after an awkward pause. I focused less on nailing the perfect line and more on keeping the dialogue going, even if it felt shaky. I started noticing the little wins—making eye contact, less fidgeting, and more laughter—even when things didn’t go perfectly. If you find yourself in a similar spot, celebrate those small shifts. They’re the real markers of progress that build your confidence, even if they seem quiet at first.
