I remember a time when I was glued to my phone, endlessly swiping without really thinking.

I’d sit there for hours, convinced I was making progress, but it felt more like background noise than actual connection. After a while, I realized I wasn’t engaged or excited; I was just numb. So, I decided to change my approach. I started setting specific times to check in—focused sessions where I’d actually look for quality interactions instead of mindlessly scrolling. I found that these short bursts led to better conversations, deeper connections, and less frustration. So if you’re feeling burnt out, take a step back and be intentional. Use those apps as they’re meant to be used: strategically and with purpose. You’ll notice a difference, trust me.
