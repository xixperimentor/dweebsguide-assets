I remember a time when I was stuck in my routine, mostly isolating myself at home, scrolling through apps.

I felt like I was waiting for some big moment to meet someone, but every attempt felt awkward and forced. The more I avoided real social interactions, the tougher it became to connect. Then I decided to switch it up. I started attending a local improv class. It wasn’t just about meeting women; it put me in a space where chatting with strangers felt natural and fun. I learned to embrace the awkwardness instead of running from it. So, if you’re in the same boat, just pick one activity that gets you around people. Whether it’s a class, a group workout, or volunteering, it can make all the difference in how you engage with the world.
