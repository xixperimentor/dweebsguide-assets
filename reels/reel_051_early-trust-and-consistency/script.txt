I remember early on, I was chatting with a woman I really liked.

I kept saying I’d call her after my weekend trip. But when I returned, I got sidetracked and didn’t reach out. A few days passed and I noticed her enthusiasm fading. It hit me that my words didn’t mean much if I couldn’t back them up. I realized that it’s not about saying the perfect thing; it’s about being dependable. So, I made a commitment to keep my promises, no matter how small. I reached out and we reconnected. If there’s one thing I learned, it’s this: being trustworthy is less about grand gestures and more about consistent, straightforward actions. Stick to what you say, and trust will grow naturally.
