I remember this time I was texting a girl I really liked.

I was so eager to keep the conversation going, I ended up cramming in a flirty comment, a reassurance, and a plan all in one message. It felt like I was juggling, but honestly, I just made things messy. She ended up responding with a short reply, and I felt confused. That’s when it hit me—each message should serve a single purpose. I realized I had dropped a bunch of signals at once, and it just crowded the conversation. From that experience, I learned to focus my texts. Now, I’ll send a warm "hey, hope you're having a good day" first, then follow up with plans later. Keep it simple and clear. Trust me, it makes a difference.
