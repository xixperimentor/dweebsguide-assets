I remember a time when I got into a disagreement with someone I was dating.

I was so focused on proving my point that I failed to notice how harsh my tone was. I could see her walls going up, and it hit me—being right didn’t matter if the conversation was pushing her away. So, I took a breath and decided to change my approach. I softened my delivery and expressed my feelings calmly. Instead of pointing fingers, I asked her how she felt about the situation. The atmosphere shifted instantly. If I could give advice to another guy, it’d be this: watch how you say things. Sometimes, it’s not just about the message but how you communicate it. A little respect in your tone can open doors to understanding that you didn’t even know were there.
