I remember my first date with Jess.

I was so focused on impressing her, trying to show off all the great things about myself. Instead of just enjoying the moment, I was mentally rehearsing lines and worrying about how I came across. A couple of awkward pauses in, I noticed she seemed a bit tense. That’s when it hit me—I was treating it like a performance instead of a conversation. I took a breath and let go of the pressure to be perfect. Once I stopped stressing, the vibe shifted. We started laughing more, and the conversation flowed much easier. If I could give one piece of advice, it would be to just relax and be real. The connection is what truly matters, not a flawless script.
