I remember this one time I asked a woman out, and she turned me down.

At first, it stung, and I found myself spiraling, questioning what was wrong with me. I was ready to take that rejection as a personal verdict on my worth. But then I realized, it wasn’t about me at all. Sometimes, it's just not the right fit, or maybe the timing was off. So, I decided to shift my focus. I started to think about what I could learn from it instead of wallowing in self-doubt. Now, if you find yourself in a similar spot, try this: take a step back. Note how you feel and ask yourself what you can take away from the experience. Rejection can teach us, but only if we let it.
