A while back, I started seeing someone new.

At first, I was all in—texts flying back and forth, planning dates, and chatting about future ideas. It felt exciting, almost like we were building something deep right from the start. But after a couple of weeks, I realized that intensity didn’t match our actual connection. She started pulling back, and I thought, "What happened?" I noticed I was rushing things without really knowing her. So, I decided to dial it down. I let things unfold more naturally, giving us both space to breathe and see if we could really trust each other. To any guy out there: slow down. Take time to really get to know someone before pushing for closeness. Let the connection grow at a pace that feels right. That’s where real trust starts.
