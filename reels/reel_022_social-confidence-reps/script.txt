I remember a time when I decided to dive into the dating scene with a big plan.

I was pumped, convinced I’d be the life of every party. But after a couple of awkward encounters, that energy faded fast. I found myself avoiding opportunities, feeling like a ghost instead of the guy I wanted to be. Then I realized I was setting the bar way too high. So, I shifted my approach. Instead of aiming to charm everyone at once, I made it a goal to just start one conversation, even if it was brief. If I could tell another guy something, it’d be this: keep it simple. Break things down until they feel manageable, even on those days when confidence seems out of reach. That’s how real growth happens.
