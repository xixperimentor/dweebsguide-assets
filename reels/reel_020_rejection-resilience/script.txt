I remember a time when I finally worked up the nerve to ask a girl out.

It was awkward, and I stumbled through my words. She smiled politely but said no. It stung, but I realized something important in that moment: rejection isn’t always a failure. I used to think discomfort meant I’d messed up, but now I see it as a sign I'm pushing my limits. Instead of wallowing, I asked myself what I could improve next time—I started focusing on just getting out there. So, I made a commitment to approach one new person every week, no matter how shaky my invitation felt. Each time, I focused on how I showed up rather than the outcome. For any guy out there, remember: every attempt teaches you something valuable.
