I remember this one date where the chemistry was off the charts.

We laughed, we clicked, everything felt electric. But a few weeks in, I started to notice something wasn't right. The effort was lopsided; I was always the one reaching out, always the one planning things. At first, I brushed it off, thinking, "Hey, maybe she’s just busy." But then I realized that I wasn’t just looking for a good vibe—I needed something deeper. So, I took a step back and reassessed. I began focusing on what truly mattered: mutual effort and shared values. If I could tell another guy one thing, it would be this: don’t mistake a spark for substance. Make sure she lifts you up, not just fills the silence.
