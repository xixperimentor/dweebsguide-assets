I remember a time when I went on a date and felt completely thrown off by how she responded to me.

I put so much weight on her reactions, constantly seeking her approval. Honestly, it was exhausting. Afterward, I took a step back and realized I was letting her feedback dictate my self-worth. That’s when it hit me: I needed to define what really mattered to me in these interactions, not just aim for a spark. So, I began to focus on my own standards—honesty, respect, and how I felt after the date, rather than just whether it led to another one. If I walked away feeling good about being myself, that was a win. If you find yourself caught up in the moment, remember to dig deeper. Set your own scorecard. It's that clarity that keeps you grounded.
