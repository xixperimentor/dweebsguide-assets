I remember chatting with a woman I really liked.

I thought I was being intense and engaging by holding this steady gaze. Instead of connecting, it felt like I was putting her through an interrogation. She looked uncomfortable, and honestly, so did I. That’s when I realized I was missing the mark. I was so focused on making eye contact that I forgot to check in with the moment. So, I took a breath, softened my approach, and let my gaze drift naturally while we talked. It was like flipping a switch. Suddenly, it felt more genuine, more fluid, and way less pressured. So, if you find yourself stuck in that intense stare, remember to ease up. Connect, listen, and let the conversation flow.
