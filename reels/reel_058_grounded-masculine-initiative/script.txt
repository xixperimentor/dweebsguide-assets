I remember a time when I thought being decisive meant being loud.

On a group hike, I kept trying to steer everyone’s choices, you know, pushing for my idea of the best trail. Instead of rallying the guys, I felt the energy shift. They started pulling away, and I realized my approach was just coming off as overbearing. Afterward, I took a step back. I focused on listening and contributing without trying to dominate. I let the others express their thoughts and offered my input calmly. It made a huge difference. Now, I’d tell any guy that it’s not about making noise; it’s about being grounded. Lean into your confidence quietly, and you’ll find it resonates more than any shout ever could.
