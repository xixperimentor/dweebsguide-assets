I remember this one time I was really into a girl.

I planned these elaborate dates, thinking they’d win her over. I was putting in all this effort, and yet, I felt like I was the only one trying. At first, I didn't see the problem. I thought, “If I just do more, she’ll come around.” But after a while, I started feeling drained, like I was pouring from an empty cup. It hit me that my efforts didn’t seem to spark any enthusiasm on her end. So, I took a step back and focused on what I truly needed. I realized it wasn’t about giving endlessly; it was about finding balance. If I had to tell another guy anything, I’d say pay attention to how your efforts are received. It should feel mutual, not like a one-way street.
