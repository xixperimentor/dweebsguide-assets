I remember going out with someone I really liked.

I was firing questions at her, trying to keep the conversation lively, but it felt forced. I could tell something was off when her responses became shorter and less engaged. It hit me—I was rushing things, not giving her space to breathe. So, I paused. I took a moment to really listen and just asked her one question that I genuinely wanted to know about her. That shift changed the whole vibe. Now I get it: curiosity should feel like an open invitation, not an interrogation. If you want her to open up, slow it down. Make her feel like she’s sharing with a friend, not under a spotlight.
