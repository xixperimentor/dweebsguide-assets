I used to think my profile was all about clever captions.

I had this one photo, where I was in a dimly lit bar, looking all mysterious. But when I chatted with women, I noticed a lot of hesitation. They’d ask questions like, "What are you really like?" It made me realize my photos weren't showing enough of who I truly am. So, I switched it up. I added a bright, genuine portrait and a full-body shot showing me out hiking. I wanted them to see the real me without doubting if that guy in the photos was the same in person. I’d tell any guy to focus on clear, relatable images. Let your photos do the talking first, so your bio can just add a little flavor. Trust me, it makes a difference.
