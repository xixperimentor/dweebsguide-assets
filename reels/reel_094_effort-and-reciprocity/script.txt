I remember this one date where I went all out.

I was texting constantly, planning elaborate get-togethers, trying to impress her. At first, I thought I was being sweet, but it felt off. Something was wrong, and I didn't know why. After a while, I realized I was pushing too hard too soon. I was trying to create a bond, but it felt more like I was chasing her approval. I stepped back and started matching my efforts to what actually existed between us. I learned that relationships need to breathe. It’s all about pacing yourself and letting things develop naturally. If I could tell another guy one thing, it’d be this: invest in the connection based on what's truly happening, not just your fears of losing it.
