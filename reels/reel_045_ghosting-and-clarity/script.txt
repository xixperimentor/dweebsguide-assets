A while back, I connected with a woman who seemed genuinely interested.

We had some great conversations, laughter—everything felt right. But then, she went quiet. I spent days overthinking her silence, wondering what I could’ve said wrong. Looking back, I realized I was searching for answers in her absence, trying to decode her every word and glance. Instead of confronting the uncertainty, I just kept cycling through the same thoughts. Now, I know better. I give one follow-up text, and if I don’t hear back, I take it as my answer. Life’s too short to linger in that gray area. If you find yourself in a similar spot, accept the message behind the silence and move forward. Trust me, it’s freeing.
