A while back, I found myself in a relationship that just felt off.

I kept making excuses for her behavior, thinking I needed to justify why I wanted to step back. I spent so much time over-explaining my feelings that I ended up feeling guilty for wanting to say no to that shaky dynamic. Then, I realized how much energy I wasted trying to please everyone. I learned to just be straightforward. I started practicing a simple, clear "no" when I needed it, without dragging on about why. It taught me that I could still show respect without offering an entire explanation. So, if you’re in a similar spot, remember: it’s okay to set boundaries without feeling the need to defend them endlessly. Just be clear and firm. That’s strength, not weakness.
