There was this time when I approached a woman at a coffee shop.

I was feeling good, but when she didn’t seem into it, I let it hit me hard. I took that rejection personally, and it messed with my head for days. Looking back, I realized I was only focused on whether she liked me. I wasn’t measuring my own effort or how genuine I was in the moment. I started to understand that it was about showing up, being respectful, and just putting myself out there. Now, I track my efforts instead of just the outcomes. I focus on initiating conversations, making invites, and maintaining my calm through it all. If I were to give advice, I’d say to value each interaction for what it is, not just whether it leads to something immediate.
