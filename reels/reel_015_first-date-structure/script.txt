I remember my first date with a girl I really liked.

We sat down, and I dove right into questions, trying to keep the conversation flowing. But I could feel this awkward energy building; it was like I was just filling in the gaps with noise. Then, I noticed how she was looking away, seeming lost in thought. It hit me—I was so eager to impress, I forgot to just let things breathe. I decided to slow down, let the silence hang for just a beat. When I did that, something shifted. She opened up more, sharing stories that connected us deeper. If I could tell another guy one thing, it’d be to embrace those pauses. They can lead to the moments that really matter.
