I remember a time when I was texting this girl I really liked.

I was eager for a response, and I found myself sending follow-ups when she didn’t reply right away. It felt like I was on edge, desperately reaching for something that just wasn’t there. Then, I took a step back. I realized I was compromising my own self-respect by chasing after her validation. So, instead of flooding her with messages, I focused on myself. I paused and gave it some time, which helped me regain clarity about what really mattered. If you find yourself in a similar spot, try this: resist the urge to chase. Check in with yourself and see if your actions are driven by desperation or confidence. That shift can make all the difference in how you connect with others.
