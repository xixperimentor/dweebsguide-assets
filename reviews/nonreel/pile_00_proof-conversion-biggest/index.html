how i design these

audio should do a different job in each family

  • testimonial reels should translate a headline result into a believable behavioral signal.
  • story strips already imply sequence, so the narration should only guide the shift, not retell a full story.
  • partner splits are contrast assets. the voice should sharpen the contrast and exit fast.
  • stats assets win on legibility. narration is optional because too much voice makes them slower and weaker.
master prompt
turn this proof asset into a short instagram reel script.

rules:
- speak in third-person brand voice, not in the member's voice
- do not pretend to be the client or invent diary details
- keep the script concrete, grounded, and low-hype
- if the asset is stats-led, default to music-only and text motion instead of narration
- if the asset is testimonial, story strip, or partner split, keep it to 2-4 short sentences
- sentence 1 should name the visible result or contrast
- sentence 2 should explain the behavioral shift or why it matters
- optional sentence 3 can anchor it as real member progress from torch method
- never use character acting, fake quotes, or claims that are not visible in the asset

audio blueprints

the corresponding script systems i suggest

these are the reusable family-level shells i would deploy across the whole 100-item proof lane.

testimonial brand narration

testimonial

goal: compress one visible result into a short trust hit that sounds calm and credible.

voice posture: steady, grounded, lightly warm, not salesy

pace: 145 to 160 wpm

template:
[headline result].
the useful part is the shift underneath it: [behavioral change].
real torch method member progress.

design notes
  • these should feel like distilled proof, not testimonials performed by an actor.
  • the first line needs to track what is plainly visible in the art.
  • avoid overclaiming the life change if the image only shows one slice of progress.
avoid
  • first-person delivery
  • fake direct quotes
  • heavy emotional acting
story strip brand narration

story strip

goal: use the implied before-and-after sequence to make progress feel observable.

voice posture: calm, observant, slightly more narrative, still restrained

pace: 140 to 155 wpm

template:
[name]'s shift here is simple: [before-state].
instead, the move became [better behavior].
that is what real progress looks like when it starts showing up in actual situations.
real torch method member progress.

design notes
  • this family can carry slightly more narration because the image already implies sequence.
  • keep the story at the pattern level unless the panel text itself gives concrete detail.
  • the value is observability, not melodrama.
avoid
  • invented scene-setting
  • sounding like a diary read
  • therapy language
partner split brand narration

partner split

goal: highlight the contrast between old pattern and better pattern without sounding preachy.

voice posture: clear, direct, slightly firmer than testimonial

pace: 145 to 160 wpm

template:
the contrast here is the point: [old pattern] versus [new pattern].
[new pattern] tends to land better because it reads as calmer and clearer.
real torch method member progress.

design notes
  • these are contrast reels, so the script should do less work than the image.
  • the voice should sharpen the difference, not restate every label on screen.
  • keep the explanation practical, not moralistic.
avoid
  • turning it into generic motivation
  • loading it with too many adjectives
  • making it sound like a lecture
stats grid music only by default

stats grid

goal: let the numbers and layout do most of the persuasion.

voice posture: if narrated, one-line cap only

pace: 130 to 145 wpm

template:
optional line only: [headline].

design notes
  • this family is usually stronger with music, movement, and readable text.
  • narration can easily clutter a stats asset and slow down comprehension.
  • only add voice if the headline needs a single clean emphasis line.
avoid
  • full paragraph narration
  • reading every number aloud
  • competing with the text density
stats music only by default

stats

goal: deliver a fast credibility pulse without slowing the viewer down.

voice posture: if narrated, clipped and minimal

pace: 130 to 145 wpm

template:
optional line only: [headline].

design notes
  • treat these as proof punctuation marks in the feed.
  • the best outcome is immediate legibility, not richer storytelling.
  • if we add voice, it should feel like a caption read, not a monologue.
avoid
  • long explanation
  • multiple claims in one read
  • extra filler
qc spot check

one canonical example per family

yes, this is a good way to qc it if you review one gold-standard example per family first, then spot-check one or two neighbors from the same family. this section is the quickest mobile check surface.

  • does line one match what is actually visible in the asset?
  • does the script add useful meaning without inventing detail or fake quotes?
  • is the length right for the family, or is it dragging past the visual?
  • does the tone sound like brand proof instead of character acting?
  • for stats assets: is narration actually helping, or should this stay music-only?
